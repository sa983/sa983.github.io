About

I am a data visualization researcher specializing in the development of compelling maps and statistical graphics using R and related computational tools.

My research focuses on the intersection of statistical methodology, cartographic design, and human-computer interaction. I am particularly interested in how visualization techniques can be employed to reveal patterns in complex datasets, with applications spanning demographic analysis, environmental monitoring, and urban planning.

I hold extensive experience in geospatial analysis, having worked with large-scale census data, satellite imagery, and temporal datasets. My methodological approach combines rigorous statistical analysis with principles of effective visual communication.

My work has been published in peer-reviewed journals and presented at international conferences. I am committed to reproducible research practices and open science, making my code and methodologies available to the broader research community.

Currently, I am exploring novel approaches to interactive visualization and the integration of machine learning techniques with traditional cartographic methods.

Projects

Selected works in data visualization and spatial analysis

Urban Demographics Mapping

R • ggplot2 • sf • leaflet

This project examines demographic patterns across urban environments using American Community Survey data. Employing advanced cartographic techniques, the analysis reveals spatial clustering of socioeconomic indicators and their temporal evolution. The methodology integrates statistical modeling with geographic information systems to produce publication-quality visualizations suitable for academic and policy applications.

Environmental Monitoring

R • tidyverse • raster • satellite imagery

A comprehensive analysis of environmental variables using satellite imagery and ground-based measurements. This research employs advanced statistical techniques to identify long-term trends in climate data. The visualization framework developed for this project has been adopted by environmental monitoring agencies for operational use.

Transportation Networks

R • igraph • network analysis • graph theory

This study applies network analysis techniques to urban transportation systems, examining connectivity patterns and identifying critical infrastructure nodes. The research combines graph theory with geospatial analysis to provide insights for transportation planning and resilience assessment.
